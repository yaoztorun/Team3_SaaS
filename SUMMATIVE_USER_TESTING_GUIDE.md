## üéØ Part 1: PIRATE Metrics User Tasks

### Task Flow Rationale
- Tasks move users through the core PIRATE funnel
- Each task represents a critical "northstar" action
- Measures **if users can complete** (usability) and **if they like it** (UTAUT)

### Task 1: Acquisition ‚Üí Activation (Signup & First Log)
**Scenario:** "You've heard about Sippin from a friend. Sign up and log your first cocktail experience."

**Success Criteria:**
- ‚úÖ Complete signup (Google OAuth or email)
- ‚úÖ Navigate to Add screen
- ‚úÖ Select a cocktail (e.g., "Mojito")
- ‚úÖ Rate it and add optional photo/caption
- ‚úÖ Post it successfully

**PIRATE Metric:** Acquisition (signup) ‚Üí Activation (first cocktail logged)

**Key Action:** First cocktail logged (key activation metric)

---

### Task 2: Activation (Social Engagement)
**Scenario:** "Explore the home feed. Like a friend's post and leave a comment on it."

**Success Criteria:**
- ‚úÖ Navigate to Home tab
- ‚úÖ View posts in feed
- ‚úÖ Like at least one post
- ‚úÖ Comment on a post

**PIRATE Metric:** Activation (meaningful engagement)

**Key Action:** First social interaction (like/comment)

---

### Task 3: Activation (Discovery Features)

- ‚úÖ Find and tap "What Can I Make"

- ‚úÖ Open a cocktail detail page

**PIRATE Metric:** Activation (feature usage)

## üìä Part 2: UTAUT Questionnaire

### Rating Scale:
- 5 = Strongly agree (Helemaal akkoord)
- 4 = Agree (Akkoord)
- 3 = Neutral (Neutraal)
- 2 = Disagree (Niet akkoord)
- 1 = Strongly disagree (Helemaal niet akkoord)

**Target:** Mean score > 3.0 for each dimension

---

### DIMENSION 1: PERFORMANCE EXPECTANCY (PE)
*"The degree to which using Sippin would enhance cocktail tracking and discovery"*

1. Using Sippin would improve my ability to track my cocktail experiences.
2. Using Sippin would help me discover new cocktails more quickly.
3. Using Sippin would increase my knowledge about cocktails.
4. I would find Sippin useful for organizing my drinking experiences.

---

### DIMENSION 2: EFFORT EXPECTANCY (EE)
*"The degree of ease associated with using Sippin"*

1. Learning how to use Sippin would be easy for me.
2. My interaction with Sippin would be clear and understandable.
3. I would find Sippin easy to use.
4. It would be easy for me to become skillful at using Sippin.

---

### DIMENSION 3: SOCIAL INFLUENCE (SI)
*"The degree to which an individual perceives that important others believe they should use Sippin"*

1. People who are important to me would think using Sippin is a good idea.
2. My friends would encourage me to use Sippin to track cocktails.
3. People whose opinions I value would support me using Sippin.
4. My friends would understand and appreciate my use of Sippin.

---

### DIMENSION 4: FACILITATING CONDITIONS (FC)
*"The degree to which an individual believes organizational and technical infrastructure exists to support use"*

1. I have the resources necessary to use Sippin (smartphone, internet).
2. Sippin is compatible with the way I currently discover and try cocktails.
3. In my area, there are enough cocktail bars/venues to make Sippin useful.
4. I would be able to get help if I had difficulties using Sippin.

---

### DIMENSION 5: BEHAVIORAL INTENTION (BI)
*"User's intention to use the technology"*

1. I intend to use Sippin in the next 3 months.
2. I predict I would use Sippin regularly in the next 3 months.
3. I plan to use Sippin frequently in the next 3 months.

## üìù Part 3: Task-Based Usability Questions

**Ask AFTER each task:**

### After Task 1 (Signup & First Log):
1. How easy was it to sign up for Sippin? (1-5 scale)
2. How easy was it to log your first cocktail? (1-5 scale)
3. Did you encounter any difficulties? What were they?
4. Was the rating and photo upload process intuitive?
5. What would make the logging process easier?

---

### After Task 2 (Social Feed Engagement):
1. Was the home feed layout clear and easy to navigate? (1-5 scale)
2. How easy was it to like and comment on posts? (1-5 scale)
3. Did you find the friend posts interesting?
4. Would you engage with friends' cocktail posts regularly?

---

### After Task 3 (What Can I Make):
1. How easy was it to find the "What Can I Make" feature? (1-5 scale)
2. How useful is this feature to you? (1-5 scale)
3. Were the ingredient selections intuitive?
4. Did the matching results meet your expectations?

---

### After Task 4 (Recipe Creation):
1. How easy was it to create your own recipe? (1-5 scale)
2. Were the ingredient input and measurement fields clear?
3. What would improve the recipe creation process?
4. Would you create more recipes in the future?

---

### After Task 5 (Profile & Stats):
1. Are the statistics displayed interesting and valuable to you? (1-5 scale)
2. Do the badges motivate you to use the app more?
3. Is your cocktail history well-organized?
4. Would you share your stats with friends?

---

### After Task 6 (Sharing):
1. How easy was it to share a cocktail post? (1-5 scale)
2. Do you think your friends would appreciate receiving these links?
3. What share method did you prefer (WhatsApp/Copy Link)?

---

### After Task 7 (Friends & Events):
1. How easy was it to find and add a friend? (1-5 scale)
2. Is the events/parties feature interesting to you?
3. Would you attend a cocktail event organized through Sippin?

---

### After Task 8 (Shop):
1. How useful is the shop feature for you? (1-5 scale)
2. Was the product information sufficient?
3. Would you actually purchase items through these links?

## üìà Part 4: Analysis Guidelines

### UTAUT Calculation Method

**All questions are positively worded** (no reverse coding needed). Higher scores = better acceptance.

#### Step-by-Step Calculation:

**For each participant:**

1. **Performance Expectancy (PE):**
   - Add scores from PE questions 1-4
   - Divide by 4
   - Example: (5 + 4 + 4 + 5) √∑ 4 = 4.5

2. **Effort Expectancy (EE):**
   - Add scores from EE questions 1-4
   - Divide by 4
   - Example: (4 + 5 + 4 + 4) √∑ 4 = 4.25

3. **Social Influence (SI):**
   - Add scores from SI questions 1-4
   - Divide by 4
   - Example: (3 + 4 + 3 + 4) √∑ 4 = 3.5

4. **Facilitating Conditions (FC):**
   - Add scores from FC questions 1-4
   - Divide by 4
   - Example: (5 + 4 + 3 + 4) √∑ 4 = 4.0

5. **Behavioral Intention (BI):**
   - Add scores from BI questions 1-3
   - Divide by 3
   - Example: (4 + 4 + 5) √∑ 3 = 4.33

**Across all participants (N = number of users):**

1. Calculate each dimension's mean across all users
2. Calculate standard deviation for each dimension
3. Report: Mean (SD)

**Example with 3 users:**

| User | PE   | EE   | SI  | FC  | BI   |
|------|------|------|-----|-----|------|
| 1    | 4.5  | 4.25 | 3.5 | 4.0 | 4.33 |
| 2    | 4.0  | 4.5  | 3.0 | 3.5 | 4.0  |
| 3    | 4.75 | 4.0  | 4.0 | 4.25| 4.67 |
| **Mean** | **4.42** | **4.25** | **3.50** | **3.92** | **4.33** |

#### Interpretation Benchmarks:

- **5.0 - 4.5:** Excellent (Strong acceptance)
- **4.49 - 4.0:** Good (Positive acceptance)
- **3.99 - 3.5:** Moderate (Cautious acceptance)
- **3.49 - 3.0:** Neutral (On the fence)
- **< 3.0:** Poor (Rejection/redesign needed)

#### Target Scores:
- **Minimum:** All dimensions > 3.0 (above neutral)
- **Goal:** PE and EE > 4.0 (users find it useful AND easy)
- **Ideal:** BI > 4.0 (strong intention to use)

### Task Success Metrics:
1. **Completion rate:** % of users who complete each task
2. **Time to complete:** Average time per task
3. **Error rate:** # of errors/wrong paths per task
4. **Satisfaction:** Post-task rating (1-5 scale)
